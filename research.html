Our Research

Exploring the frontiers of safe and responsible AI development

AI Safety + Science

We aim to build a unified red teaming/evaluation platform for AI4Science models, in collaboration with Lawrence Livermore National Lab, SAND, Open Philanthropy, and the Center of AI Safety.

This effort will be joined with the trillion parameter consortium (TPC), which aims to train large language models for science data. Our project will enable safety evaluations for these models, such as whether an LLM might provide procedures to generate controlled chemical substances.

  • Safety evaluations for large-scale scientific models
  • Prevention of misuse in chemistry and biology
  • Collaborative research with national laboratories

AI Safety + Healthcare

This research direction aims to optimize trustworthy models built for predictions and recommendations in medicine and healthcare.

We ensure model transparency and interoperability to ground the predictions in this safety-critical domain. Our work focuses on developing AI systems that can provide reliable medical insights while maintaining patient privacy and adhering to ethical guidelines.

  • Trustworthy and explainable AI for medical diagnosis
  • Privacy-preserving healthcare analytics
  • Bias mitigation in clinical decision support systems

AI Safety + Economics

We propose novel mechanism designs and risk assessments for the usage of AI in economic activities.

As generative AIs increasingly master content creation, we investigate how this power will influence humans' content creation and art production. We aim to ensure symbiosis between human creativity and AI creativity, and to design appropriate content creator incentives to induce desirable human content creation.

  • Economic value assessment of data for ML models
  • Incentive mechanisms for human-AI creative collaboration
  • Market design for AI-generated content
